Key Cohort Observations
Patterns worth acting on
M+0 ACTIVATION GAP
Only 51% of sellers sell in their own go-live month. More than half go live but don't make a sale until the next month — or never. First-sale activation is the #1 lever.
M+1 JUMP
Retention jumps to 78% at M+1 — sellers who make it past their first month are far more likely to continue. The M+0→M+1 window is critical.
M+3 CLIFF
Retention plateaus around 40–56% after M+3. If a seller survives 3 months, they tend to stick around. Interventions after M+3 have diminishing returns — focus effort before this point.
05 — Gross Active Pool & Running Churn per Cohort
Gross Active = cumulative unique sellers who have sold at least once up to that month (pool grows as new sellers activate). MoM Churn = sellers who sold last month but didn't sell this month — revolving door model: if a seller churns, comes back, then churns again, they are counted each time. Still Active = sellers who actually sold that month. Toggle between counts and percentages (% of gross active pool).
